What to Expect: June to August 2026

This entire period falls within Nigeria's peak rainy season. The goal is not to find a rain-free date — it is to find the date with the most manageable rain.

Why August is Not Recommended
The "August Break" Is Unreliable
⚠ NiMet rates Anambra's August break as MILD every reviewed year
⚠ In 2022 — NO break occurred. Historic flooding hit Anambra
⚠ In 2023 and 2024 — only mild dry spells in the southeast
⚠ The break is strongest in Lagos/Ogun — NOT in Anambra
⚠ Climate change is weakening it further each year
August Risk Factors for This Event
✗ August Meeting conflicts with guest availability
✗ New Yam Festival — community focus is elsewhere
✗ Flood risk on major routes — PH, Aba, Onitsha roads
✗ All-day persistent rain disrupts ceremonial rites
✗ Anambra flood season peaks in August — venue risk
Planning Essentials — Regardless of Date

No date in this period is rain-free. These measures are non-negotiable for any weekend chosen.

🏗   Venue & Infrastructure
  • Covered marquee tents or hall — size for full attendance
  • Interlock/concrete flooring — avoid bare soil
  • Proper drainage channels around venue
🔊   Technical Setup
  • Weatherproof all sound systems and power supply
  • Elevated cabling to minimise surface flood risk
  • Generator backup — power interruptions common in rain
📋   Programme Design
  • All key outdoor rites scheduled before 1pm
  • 30–45 min rain-delay buffer built into schedule
  • Indoor fallback segment for every outdoor activity
📡   Monitoring
  • Check NiMet forecast 7–10 days before event
  • Assign weather monitor on each event day
  • Brief coordinators on rain contingency plan
